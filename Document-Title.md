Today, Tattooville encompasses a diverse group of individuals who share a passion for tattooing, body modification, and the creative process.

**History of Tattooville Culture**
---------------------------------

The history of Tattooville culture is closely tied to the evolution of tattoo art itself. In the early 20th century, tattoos were often associated with marginalized groups, such as sailors, circus performers, and prisoners. However, as tattooing became more accepted, it also attracted a new wave of artists who sought to push the boundaries of this art form.

During the 1970s and 1980s, Tattooville culture began to take shape, with the emergence of tattoo conventions, shops, and publications dedicated to the art. This period saw the rise of iconic figures like Norman "Sailor Jerry" Collins and Don Ed Hardy, who helped establish tattoos as a legitimate form of artistic expression.
